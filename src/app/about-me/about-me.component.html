G'day - I'm Justin!

I'm a recent Bachelor of Information Technology (User Experience Design) graduate from the University of Queensland, Australia. I also studied as an exchange student at Purdue University, where I was heavily involved in the Game Development faculty. I was a member of several clubs and societies, including UQ Design Society, Purdue's SIGGRAPH chapter, Asian American Association, and Purdue Game Developers United.

Right now I work at the Australian Taxation Office as a full-time web developer, conducting development and testing for ATOOnline. I have also been involved and exposed to other topics such as automation testing, while serving as a proxy Scrum Master and team UX champion. This has given me first-hand experience of how development is conducted in a large, professional organisation.

I've also worked in several areas inside and around user experience design, such as physical computing and social/mobile computing, as well as other areas game design and development, technical art, film editing and 3D modelling. Human-computer interaction has always fascinated me - I love to learn about how so much we take for granted in software is manipulated by seemingly minor changes in hardware, user interface, visuals or architecture. I have most recently been fascinated with 3D level design from both a user experience and game development standpoint, and how the manipulation of composition, colour and lighting can be used to convey tone, theme, gameplay or story.

As I've worked in such a variety of problem areas, I believe I have the experience and adaptability to bring value to any organisation. I devote much of my spare time outside of work to learning and development, whether that be learning Angular and UI programming, working on my roleplaying game At the Edge of the West, or working through my large backlog of GDC talks.
